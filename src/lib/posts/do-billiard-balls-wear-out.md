Like with any hobby or sport we put money into, we want to know how long our equipment will last. The same can be said for us pool players who have put hundreds, if not thousands of dollars into our equipment, billiard balls included. And because billiard balls take so much abuse at the table, its natural to wonder it they’ll ever wear out. So do they?

<strong>Yes, billiard balls can wear out over time, although the rate at which this happens depends on various factors including the quality of the balls, the material they are made from, how frequently they are used, and how well they are maintained. Here are some ways billiard balls can wear out:</strong>

## Material Degradation

## Material Degradation

<li><strong>Phenolic Resin Balls (like [Aramith](/are-aramith-pool-balls-worth-it/)):</strong> High-quality billiard balls are made from phenolic resin, which is very durable. However, they can still degrade over time, showing signs of yellowing, chipping, or cracking.</li> <li><strong>Polyester or Acrylic Balls:</strong> Lower-quality balls made from polyester or acrylic are more susceptible to wear and tear, including surface scratches and color fading.</li>

## Surface Wear

## Surface Wear

<li><strong>Chips and Cracks:</strong> Billiard balls can chip or even crack if they are dropped or hit with extreme force.</li> <li><strong>Scratches:</strong> The balls can also accumulate minor scratches on their surface, affecting their roll.</li> <li><strong>Gloss Loss:</strong> The shiny surface can become dull over time.</li>

## Shape Deformation

## Shape Deformation

With extended use, billiard balls can become slightly out-of-round, which can affect their roll and playability.

## Color Fading

## Color Fading

Especially in the case of lower-quality balls or those exposed to sunlight, the colors can fade over time.
