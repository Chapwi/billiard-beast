It’s not possible for everyone to have a full-size pool table in their home. Some folks just don’t have the space to fit a 7 or 8-foot pool table comfortably in their house or apartment. And while most people agree that full-size pool tables are best, there are plenty of smaller pool tables on the market. So, this begs the question: Are mini pool tables worth it?

<strong>Mini pool tables are great for some people, but not for others. In general, a miniature pool table is better than no pool table at all. However, those who are used to playing on full-size tables find it hard to “downgrade” to a mini pool table. But their low cost and high portability make mini pool tables a great option for many people. It’s also a great way to work on your game at home, if you are limited by space or budget.</strong>

## Main Features

If you’re unsure about whether to buy a mini pool table, read on. This article is a complete guide to mini pool tables, and it includes everything you need to know to make an informed decision. Plus, there’s even a recommendation for the best mini pool table at the end!

Mini pool tables can provide hours of fun for the whole family. They’re exactly what they sound like: miniature versions of larger pool tables found in bars and pool halls. The key concepts remain the same on mini pool tables as they are on larger tables. It’s still all about accuracy, angles, power, and finesse.

## Complete Set

Pretty much any game that can be played on a full-size table can be played on a mini pool table. Games like 8-ball, 9-ball, cutthroat, 3-ball, and others provide plenty of fun for kids and adults.

The nice thing about this is that you can find a size that will work well for the space you have available. Of course, the larger the table, the more “authentic” the actual gameplay will feel. But that doesn’t mean that you should go for the biggest table you can fit in your place. Instead, it’s a good idea to take a look at the different kinds of mini pool tables out there to see if one type will be best for your particular situation over the other types.

## Compact and Portable

Foldable tables are convenient because they are easily stored. They are designed with legs that can fold under the playing surface, much like the legs of a card table. These types of tables are perhaps the most popular not only because they are easy to store and transport, but because they come in larger sizes up to 6-feet.

Standard mini tables are essentially just smaller versions of those pool tables found in pool halls and bars. Their legs won’t fold in and they can’t be placed on tables (unless you’re super tall and can reach!). They are ideal for those people who are looking for a classic-style pool table but they don’t have enough room for a bigger one.

## Quick Setup

Some of the larger mini pool tables do use standard 2.25-inch billiard balls on their sets. But this is only common among 6-foot tables. 4-foot mini tables often use 1.5-inch balls.

As the tables get smaller, it only makes sense to downsize the balls as well. After all, a set of regular billiard balls would take up too much space on a small 20-inch tabletop table.

## Pro-Style Play

The smaller the table, the smaller the balls will be. And, for that matter, the smaller the pockets will be, as well. But this is a good thing. If you can get used to playing with smaller balls on a smaller table, it won’t be a big jump up to a full-size table eventually. This is why many people buy their kids a mini pool table and then upgrade when their kids are big enough to play on a regular table.

With so many different types of mini pool tables, and the widely varying quality accompanying them, it’s hard to answer definitively how long mini pool tables last. The best we can do is give you a ballpark estimate.

## Room Size Recommendations

As with pretty much any product, the more you spend on it, the longer it’s likely to last. And the better you care for it, the longer it will last, guaranteed. The nice thing is that most mini pool tables are not expensive, so it’s not that big a deal when it doesn’t last more than 2 years or so.

In the world of full-size pool tables, the best ones have playing surfaces made of slate, which is a type of rock. The slate provides a flat, smooth surface on which the balls can roll with minimum friction. Slate tables last years and years because they’re impervious to warping due to moisture and humidity.

## Exclusive Community Access

Mini pool tables, on the other hand, aren’t made with slate. Instead, some kind of medium-density fiberboard (MDF) is used to make the playing surface. And even with protective coatings on the fiberboard, it’s still wood. And wood will warp when exposed to enough moisture. But, when we’re talking about mini pool tables, is a slightly warped surface really such a big deal?

Ideally, we would want the playing surface on our mini pool table to be completely flat and true. After all, we want the balls to move consistently on every game so we can get good by developing our skills. However, most people with mini pool tables are more concerned with entertainment than developing skill.

## Pros and Cons

This is a bit of a conundrum for mini pool table owners. Of course, most people want to get better at anything they do, including playing pool on a mini pool table. But if they’re more concerned with having a fun time, a warped playing surface won’t make play impossible. In fact, it can often add a new challenge, forcing players to adapt to the warped surface.

This is especially true for kids playing on a mini table. Chances are they won’t care if the table gets a little warped over time. So it’s good to try and get the best mini pool table you can for your budget, but depending on who’s using it, you may still get several years of use out of it even if it does warp.

## Final Thoughts

While we’re on the subject of slate, let’s talk about how much mini pool tables weigh. Slate is the major component that makes full-size pool tables so heavy, so you may not be surprised to learn that mini pool tables are, on average, incredibly light.

The smallest tabletop pool tables weigh under 10 pounds. Mid-size mini pool tables usually weigh between 25 and 50 pounds. The largest of the mini tables, those that are around 6-feet, generally weigh somewhere between 100 and 150 pounds.
