## Felt vs. Cloth: Understanding the Terminology

Before we go further, let's clarify something that confuses many pool table owners: **felt and cloth are not the same thing**, though the terms are often used interchangeably in casual conversation.

**Cloth** is the technically correct term for the woven fabric covering your pool table. It's made from natural and synthetic fibers and is engineered specifically for billiards.

**Felt** is actually a different material. Aa non-woven fabric made by matting fibers together, primarily used in hats and crafts. It's thicker and less consistent than pool table cloth, which makes it unsuitable for serious play.

However, industry convention and common usage have blurred these lines. Most manufacturers and retailers use "pool table felt" and "pool table cloth" interchangeably when referring to the woven fabric covering your table. For the purposes of this guide, we'll follow this standard terminology, but rest assured that when discussing billiards, we're talking about high-quality woven cloth, not actual felt.

## Types of Pool Table Cloth: Woolen vs. Worsted

The two primary types of pool table cloth are woolen and worsted, and understanding the differences between them is important to making the right choice for your table.

### Woolen Cloth

Woolen pool table cloth is made from naturally carded wool fibers that are loosely woven together. The fibers aren't combed, which leaves the surface slightly fuzzy or napped.

**Characteristics of Woolen Cloth:**

- **Speed**: Woolen cloth is slower than worsted. The nap causes more friction, which slows down the cue ball and object balls
- **Feel**: It has a softer, slightly rougher feel under your hand
- **Durability**: Wool is resilient and bounces back well, but the nap can show wear patterns over time
- **Price**: Woolen cloth is generally more affordable, making it a good choice for recreational players
- **Best for**: Players who prefer a slower game, home recreational tables, and budget-conscious buyers

Woolen cloth is forgiving for developing players because it naturally slows the game down, giving you more time to process and execute shots. However, many competitive players find it frustrating because the inconsistent nap can cause unpredictable ball behavior.

### Worsted Cloth

Worsted pool table cloth is made from wool that's been combed before weaving, aligning the fibers in the same direction. This creates a smooth, consistent playing surface.

**Characteristics of Worsted Cloth:**

- **Speed**: Worsted is faster and more responsive. The smooth surface reduces friction and allows the balls to travel farther with the same stroke
- **Feel**: It has a smooth, polished feel that many advanced players prefer
- **Durability**: The aligned fibers make worsted more resistant to visible wear patterns and matting
- **Price**: Worsted cloth commands a premium price, often costing 30-50% more than woolen options
- **Best for**: Competitive play, tournament tables, serious enthusiasts, and professional establishments

Worsted cloth provides more consistent ball response and faster play. Professional tournaments almost universally use worsted cloth because it offers predictability and allows players to develop more refined technique.

### Wool Blend Alternatives

Some manufacturers offer cloth made from wool blends (typically wool mixed with nylon or acrylic). These aim to provide a middle ground. Bbetter speed and consistency than pure wool at a lower price point than premium worsted.

## Pool Table Felt Color Selection: Does It Matter?

One of the most visible decisions you'll make is choosing the color of your pool table cloth. Beyond aesthetics, you might wonder: does color actually affect play?

### The Performance Question

**Short answer**: Color itself doesn't directly affect ball physics or play performance. A ball rolls the same way on tournament green as it does on blue or red.

**However, color does affect visibility and eye strain**, which indirectly influences performance:

- **Tournament Green**: The traditional choice for professional and tournament play. The specific shade of green (often called "tournament green" or "club green") was historically chosen because it complements the white cue ball and provides excellent contrast. Research suggests this color reduces eye strain during extended play sessions
- **Navy Blue**: Becoming increasingly popular in modern installations. The deep blue provides excellent contrast and is preferred by some players for reduced glare
- **Burgundy/Wine Red**: Another tournament-approved option that some players find reduces eye fatigue compared to green
- **Red/Bright Red**: Less common but used in some recreational settings. Some players find bright red fatiguing during long sessions
- **Black**: Dramatic looking but generally not recommended. The color absorbs light and can make it harder to track ball movement

### Practical Considerations

When selecting color, consider:

1. **Lighting in Your Space**: Bright colors like red may work against poor lighting, while deeper colors like green or navy work better in standard lighting conditions
2. **Decor Integration**: Choose a color that complements your game room aesthetic without compromising playability
3. **Tournament Requirements**: If you plan to play in sanctioned tournaments, stick with tournament-approved colors (green, navy, or burgundy)
4. **Personal Preference**: After accounting for practical concerns, choose the color that makes you happiest. Yyou'll be staring at it often

**Standard Recommendation**: Tournament green remains the safest choice, offering proven performance and wide acceptance in competitive settings. Navy blue is an excellent alternative that many modern players prefer.

## How to Measure Your Table for Felt

Before purchasing cloth, you need accurate measurements of your table to ensure proper fit and avoid costly mistakes.

### Taking the Right Measurements

**Step 1: Measure the Playing Surface**

The playing surface is the area within the rails (the actual slate bed where balls roll). Measure both length and width:

- Place a measuring tape across the playing surface from the inner edge of one rail to the inner edge of the opposite rail
- Record measurements to the nearest 1/8 inch
- Take multiple measurements to ensure accuracy
- Record both length and width

**Step 2: Note Your Table Dimensions**

Pool tables come in standard sizes:

- **7-foot table**: 39" × 78" playing surface (common for home use)
- **8-foot table**: 44" × 88" playing surface (standard for leagues and tournaments)
- **9-foot table**: 50" × 100" playing surface (professional tournament standard)
- **Snooker tables**: 6' × 12' (much larger, specialized cloth)

**Step 3: Account for Rail Height**

The cloth must cover the playing surface and wrap around the rails. Measure the height of your rails from the playing surface to the top edge:

- Measure at least 3-4 points around the table (rails can vary slightly)
- Record the average height
- Standard rail height is typically 1.5" to 2"

### Pro Tips for Accurate Measurement

- **Get a Helper**: It's easier with two people. Oone to hold the measuring tape while the other records measurements
- **Use the Same Measuring Tool**: Stick with one measuring tape throughout to avoid inconsistencies
- **Photograph Your Table**: Take clear photos of your table from multiple angles for the installer to reference
- **Note Any Damage**: Mark any areas where the current cloth is damaged, worn, or installed incorrectly. Tthis information helps the installer

### When to Call a Professional Measurer

If your table is an unusual size, antique, or custom-built, consider hiring a professional pool table technician to measure it. They can identify potential installation challenges that affect cloth choice and ensure you order the correct amount of material.

## DIY vs. Professional Installation: Making the Right Choice

Once you've selected your cloth, you face another decision: should you install it yourself or hire a professional?

### Professional Installation: The Smart Choice

**Advantages:**

- **Expertise**: Professionals have years of experience ensuring proper fit, tension, along with also alignment
- **Proper Tools**: They have specialized tools (stretching equipment, stapling guns, corner form tools) that aren't worth buying for a one-time project
- **Warranty**: Most professionals guarantee their work, so if something goes wrong, it's their responsibility
- **Speed**: A professional installs felt in 2-4 hours; DIY can take 2-3 days
- **Quality Results**: Professional installation ensures consistent tension and eliminates wrinkles, ensuring optimal play

**Disadvantages:**

- **Cost**: Professional installation typically costs $300-$800, depending on table size and your location
- **Scheduling**: You need to arrange a service visit and time when someone is available

### DIY Installation: A Challenging Option

**Advantages:**

- **Cost Savings**: You save the labor fee, potentially saving $300-$800
- **Control**: You control the schedule and can take your time
- **Learning Experience**: You'll understand your table's construction better

**Disadvantages:**

- **Very Difficult**: Installing cloth properly requires skill and experience. Mistakes are common
- **Tool Requirement**: You'll need to rent or purchase stretching equipment, industrial stapling guns, and other specialized tools ($200-$400+)
- **Time-Intensive**: Expect 2-3 full days of work, and that's if you don't make mistakes
- **Frustration**: The wrinkles, uneven tension, and alignment issues that plague DIY installations are extremely difficult to fix
- **Permanent Mistakes**: Poor installation can damage your slate or make future professional installation more expensive
- **Playing Surface Affected**: Even slightly poor installation noticeably affects ball roll and play consistency

### Honest Assessment: Who Should DIY?

DIY installation makes sense only if:

1. You're experienced with similar technical projects
2. You have access to proper tools
3. You have 2-3 full days available
4. You're willing to accept the risk of a less-than-perfect result
5. The cost savings justify the effort and potential problems

**For Most Players**: Professional installation is the clear winner. The difference between professional and DIY installation is immediately obvious when you play on the table. Professional installation ensures your new cloth performs as intended and lasts as long as possible.

**Industry Recommendation**: Even if you're a skilled DIYer, professional installation is worth the investment to guarantee optimal playability and cloth performance.

## How to Maintain and Clean Pool Table Felt

Proper maintenance extends the life of your cloth and keeps it playing optimally. Here's how to care for your investment:

### Regular Cleaning Routine

**Weekly Maintenance:**

- **Brush the Cloth**: Use a soft, fine-bristled pool table brush (available at any billiards retailer)
  - Brush in straight lines perpendicular to the long axis of the table
  - Brush from the center outward toward the rails
  - Do this before and after play sessions
  - This removes dust and chalk particles that accumulate during play

- **Use a Vacuum**: A portable handheld vacuum (not a full-sized household vacuum) can remove debris
  - Gently vacuum the playing surface and under the rails
  - Use the upholstery brush attachment to avoid damaging the cloth
  - Never use aggressive suction

**Monthly Deep Clean:**

- **Spot Clean Stains**: If you spill a drink or get a stain
  - Blot (don't rub) the spot with a clean, slightly damp cloth
  - Use only water or specialized pool table cloth cleaner
  - Never use harsh chemicals or bleach
  - Allow to air dry completely

### What NOT to Do

**Never:**

- Pour liquids directly on the cloth (accidents happen. Mminimize damage by blotting immediately)
- Use household cleaners, alcohol, or bleach
- Eat or drink heavily near the table (this isn't just about the cloth. Iit's about respect for the equipment)
- Sit or lean heavily on the table
- Drag items across the cloth
- Use abrasive brushes or steel wool
- Apply fabric softeners or conditioners designed for clothing

### Professional Cleaning

Consider professional deep cleaning every 1-2 years:

- Professional cleaners use specialized equipment and solutions that home equipment can't match
- They can restore some vibrancy to faded cloth
- They can address stubborn stains without damaging the cloth
- Cost is typically $150-$300, well worth the investment for premium cloth

### Preventive Measures

The best maintenance is prevention:

- **No Food or Drinks**: Enforce a strict no-food/no-drink policy during play (water in a sealed container is okay)
- **Cue Control**: Teach proper cue technique to avoid tears from violent breaks or missed hits
- **Table Cover**: Use a heavy-duty fitted table cover when the table isn't in use to protect from dust and accidental damage
- **Controlled Environment**: Keep the room temperature and humidity relatively stable. Eextreme fluctuations can cause cloth to expand or contract, affecting play

## When to Replace Your Pool Table Felt: Signs of Wear

Even with excellent maintenance, pool table cloth eventually wears out and needs replacement. Here are the signs it's time for new cloth:

### Visible Wear Patterns

**Worn Paths**: If you notice distinct paths worn into the cloth where balls frequently travel, it's time to replace:

- These paths develop on the spots where players frequently practice (especially along the center line and near the pockets)
- The worn areas become slick and change ball behavior
- Once these paths are visible, they're impossible to repair

**Matting and Napping**:

- The surface becomes dull or flat instead of maintaining its original nap
- The cloth feels slick in some areas and rough in others
- This indicates the fibers are breaking down

### Ball Performance Changes

**Inconsistent Roll**:

- The cue ball and object balls behave unpredictably
- Identical shots produce different results
- The cloth no longer provides consistent friction

**Increased Speed Variation**:

- Balls roll significantly faster or slower than they used to
- This is often caused by uneven wear or cloth compression

**Pilling or Pilling Spots**:

- Small balls of fiber form on the surface (more common with lower-quality cloth)
- These affect ball behavior and are impossible to repair

### Physical Damage

**Tears or Holes**:

- Any tear means replacement is necessary. Rrepairs are visible and affect play
- Tears often occur near the break spot or from aggressive play

**Water Damage**:

- Spills that soak through require cloth replacement
- Wet cloth leads to mildew, mold, along with also structural damage
- Never attempt to dry a wet cloth with heat. Tthis damages the fibers

**Cigarette Burns or Stains**:

- Permanent discoloration that can't be cleaned
- These are particularly relevant in establishments that allowed smoking

### Timeline Expectations

**How Long Does Pool Table Cloth Last?**

- **Professional/Tournament Use**: Cloth in heavy-use professional settings is typically replaced annually or every 18 months
- **League Play**: Cloth used in league matches lasts 2-3 years
- **Home Recreational Use**: Cloth on a home table used 2-3 times per week typically lasts 5-10 years
- **Casual Use**: Tables used occasionally may have cloth that lasts 10-15+ years

**Factors Affecting Lifespan:**

- Quality of cloth (premium Simonis lasts longer than budget options)
- Frequency of use (more play means faster wear)
- Maintenance quality (well-maintained cloth lasts significantly longer)
- Environmental conditions (extreme humidity or temperature fluctuations accelerate wear)

### Making the Replacement Decision

**Replace Immediately If:**

- There are visible tears
- Ball behavior has become unpredictable
- The cloth shows water damage or mold
- Worn paths are clearly visible

**Consider Replacement If:**

- The cloth is 5+ years old and shows moderate wear
- Cleaning no longer restores appearance
- You're preparing for league play or competitive participation
- You notice consistent play issues that professional techs attribute to cloth condition

## FAQ

**What's the main difference between woolen and worsted pool table cloth?**

Woolen cloth is slower and rougher with a napped surface, making it forgiving for developing players. Worsted cloth is faster and smoother with aligned fibers, preferred by competitive and tournament players for consistent ball response.

**Should I choose a professional installer or install pool table felt myself?**

Professional installation is recommended for most players. It costs $300-$800 but guarantees proper tension, alignment, along with also optimal play. DIY installation is difficult, time-consuming, and often produces wrinkles and uneven tension that affect gameplay.

**Does the color of pool table cloth affect how the balls play?**

Color doesn't affect ball physics, but it affects visibility and eye strain. Tournament green is the standard choice for reduced eye fatigue and excellent contrast with the white cue ball. Navy blue is a modern alternative many players prefer.

**What should I do if my pool table felt develops wrinkles?**

Minor wrinkles at edges can sometimes be reduced by a professional technician. Major wrinkles typically require re-stretching the entire cloth, costing $200-$500. Often it's more economical to replace the cloth entirely.

**How long will my pool table cloth last if I take good care of it?**

With regular maintenance and casual home use (2-3 times weekly), quality cloth lasts 5-10 years. Professional use requires annual replacement. Premium brands like Simonis last longer than budget alternatives.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Pool Table Cloth

### Q: Can I paint my pool table cloth?

**A:** No. Painting or dyeing pool table cloth is not recommended. Paint or dye will affect the cloth's friction properties, ruining the playing surface. If you want to change colors, replacement is the only option.

### Q: How much does it cost to replace pool table felt?

**A:** The total cost typically ranges from $500-$1,500:
- Cloth material: $200-$700 (depending on brand and table size)
- Professional installation: $300-$800
- Budget options start around $400-$600; premium installations can exceed $2,000

### Q: Can I use a pool table if the felt is torn?

**A:** Not recommended for any serious play. Even small tears affect ball behavior and ball control. Professional repair is possible but expensive. Rreplacement is usually more economical.

### Q: What's the difference between 7-foot and 9-foot table cloth?

**A:** The cloth is the same material; the size is simply different. 9-foot cloth requires more material and more installation time, so it costs more (typically $100-$200 more than 7-foot cloth).

### Q: Can I use a pool table with wet cloth?

**A:** Absolutely not. A wet cloth is unpredictable and can cause mold and mildew growth. Never play on wet cloth, and if cloth gets wet, allow it to dry completely (48+ hours) before use. If it doesn't dry properly, professional replacement may be necessary to prevent mold.

### Q: Is there a way to stretch out wrinkles without replacing the cloth?

**A:** Minor wrinkles near the edges can sometimes be reduced by a professional technician, but major wrinkles or creases require re-stretching the entire cloth. This typically costs $200-$500 and almost always makes economic sense to just replace the cloth.

### Q: Should I cover my pool table when not in use?

**A:** Yes. A heavy-duty fitted table cover protects the cloth from dust, accidental damage, and fading. Covers typically cost $50-$150 and significantly extend cloth life.

### Q: Does humidity affect pool table cloth?

**A:** Yes. High humidity can cause cloth to expand slightly, while low humidity causes contraction. Extreme fluctuations can affect how the cloth sits and how balls roll. Maintain relatively stable room conditions (40-50% humidity is ideal) to minimize these effects.

### Q: Can pool table cloth be patched?

**A:** Visible patches look obviously patched and affect play. While professional patching is possible, most players prefer replacement. Patches are only typically used for temporary repairs until replacement is possible.

### Q: What's the best way to break in new cloth?

**A:** New cloth is slightly stiff and needs to be played on to properly "settle in." Plan for the first 50-100 games to feel slightly different than typical play. By 200 games, the cloth will have settled and be performing optimally. Don't worry about light wear during this break-in period. Iit's normal.
